# FAA AVS Portfolio Overview

## Portfolio Summary

The FAA Aviation Safety (AVS) portfolio consists of systems supporting airmen certification, medical certification, designee management, and document management. This research covers the systems undergoing modernization as part of the CARES initiative.

## Systems in Scope

| System | Purpose | Status |
|--------|---------|--------|
| RMS | Records Management System — stores 174M TIFF airmen images | Being replaced by CARES |
| MedXPress/MSS | Medical certification application and adjudication | Active, Login.gov migration underway |
| IACRA | Integrated Airman Certification and Rating Application | Active, absorbed into CARES Phase 2 |
| DMS | Designee Management System — manages 13 designee types | Active, Login.gov migration underway |
| CARES | Cloud-based replacement platform | In development, Phase 1 FOC slipped to Fall 2027 |

## Modernization Direction

CARES (Certification and Airmen Records Enterprise System) is the cloud-based successor platform replacing or absorbing RMS, and eventually IACRA. It integrates MyAccess, DocuSign, and Pay.gov.

## Key Cross-Cutting Concerns

- Identity: Migrating from legacy IWA/CAIS auth to Login.gov
- Document management: TIFF-heavy legacy formats transitioning to cloud storage
- Data retention: Up to 50-year retention requirements (MedXPress/MSS)
- Security: FIPS HIGH classification on medical systems

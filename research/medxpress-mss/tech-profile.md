# MedXPress / MSS — Medical Support Systems

## Overview
MedXPress is the applicant-facing portal, but it's one of six subsystems under the MSS umbrella:
- **MedXPress** — applicant intake (medxpress.faa.gov)
- **AMCS** — AME exam processing (amcs.faa.gov)
- **DIWS** — document imaging & workflow (internal, PIV-gated)
- **CPDSS** — covered-position decision support (internal)
- **CHAPS/EMRS** — clinic health program (being replaced by EMRS)
- **DSS** — decision support / research archive (no PII)

## Volume
- 400,000+ medical certificate applications annually
- ~5,000 AMEs use AMCS
- 25 documents per exam upload limit, 3MB each
- Accepted formats: PDF, DOC, DOCX, JPG, JPEG, XPS

## Identifier Taxonomy
| Identifier | Generated By | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmation Number | MedXPress | Short-lived handoff to AME |
| Applicant ID | AMCS | Lifetime unique identifier |
| MID (Medical ID) | MedXPress | Unique per exam |
| PI Number | Assigned on pathology | Lifetime pathology marker |
| SSN/pseudo-SSN | Voluntary | Deduplication |
| AME Serial Number | FAA | AME identity |

## Data Elements
**MedXPress (applicant):** Class, name, DOB, SSN, address, phone, citizenship, hair/eye color, sex, prior cert info, occupation, employer, pilot time, medication use, full medical history checkboxes, health professional visits, arrest history

**AMCS (AME exam):** Height, weight, 20+ body systems, vision/hearing, BP/pulse, urine, ECG, disposition (issue/deny/defer)

**AMCS Document Types:** ECGs, IDs, lab reports, discharge summaries, narratives, psychiatric evals, CogScreen, cardiac test reports, court documents, correspondence, and many specialty categories

## SORNs & Retention
- DOT/FAA 856 — Airmen Medical Records
- Retention: 50 years after case closed (NARA N1-237-05-005)
- Form: FAA 8500-8 (OMB 2120-0034)

## Modernization Opportunity
Not "replace MedXPress" — collapse the layered document/status model across MedXPress → AMCS → DIWS → CPDSS → CHAPS into one case architecture with shared identity, document storage, notification, and status model.
